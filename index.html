MY DESIGN PROCESS
  • discover and research
  • problem and scope
  • ideate and sketch
  • design and prototype
  • test and iterate
  • handover and monitor

I start by digging into user needs and the market, using interviews and analysis to identify the main challenges. This groundwork shapes every design decision I make, ensuring I’m truly addressing user and business goals.

Next, I translate my research into clear problem statements and user personas. This step helps me outline the project’s scope and goals, serving as a roadmap for the entire design process.

With a solid direction in place, I brainstorm and sketch out a wide range of possible solutions. This is where I encourage creativity, pushing beyond obvious ideas and landing on multiple concepts that can move forward.

I then transform the most promising concepts into interactive or tangible prototypes, allowing me to test and refine ideas before investing significant resources. It’s a hands-on way to see what works and gather early feedback.

Armed with user feedback and real-world data, I refine the design to ensure it’s easy to use and truly meets user needs. This continuous improvement cycle helps me stay aligned with the target audience.

Finally, I collaborate with developers to bring my design to life and then monitor its performance post-launch. I make ongoing improvements and optimizations to keep the product on track and growing over time.
